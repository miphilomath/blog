The scope of improvement!

July 25, 2022 2-minute read
Others • insights

When someone says, they have mastered some skill/concept/subject and agrees, that they are perfect in that particular domain. They are wrong!

There is always some scope for improvement. We can always improve our skills, knowledge, personality, projects and everything. But how do one know where is one lacking and needs improvement? How can one tell if they have really mastered something?

That is simple. Answer the most simple questions we avoid on daily basis and you will know if you really understand the thing you think you know.

This will also help you find the gaps in your knowledge in basics and really master those concepts. But even masters also learn something everyday. So, ask yourself the questions you think you can answer daily. This will help your brain find some new neural pathways. If you think, you can answer something, I suggest you answer them, in the simplest words possible, via blog, talks, lessons, tutorials, anything.

If you can’t think of the question to ask yourself, try to question everything in “What is …” format or the following questionings. I promise, something new will come up on the journey.

Following patterns will instigate your mind to think deeper:

  • What is …?
  • How … works?
  • Why … exists?
  • What if …?

These are the example for these questions.

  • What is rainbow?
  • What is cpu?
  • What is a process?
  • What is a thread?

The list is endless. This experiment will help you learn a lot more and truly understand the thing, you think you know.

So, in conclusion, there is always some scope for improvement. And, this is the reason, human race have advanced beyond the perfection of history and are still advancing reaching for the new perfection. This is also a note to all the perfectionist out there, you can’t be perfect on your first try and if you will be, there will always be scope for improvement which contradicts your efforts in the very beginning of producing something perfect. So start working and improve over time.

I follow the quote by Einstein to know what I don’t understand.

If you can’t explain it to a six year old, you don’t understand it yourself.
