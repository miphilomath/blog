Brain Overclocked?

March 6, 2019 2-minute read

In the last blog post, I referred, over thinking might be one of the reason that stops me to write me more and more blog posts. And in this post, I am going to discuss on that.

Never memorize something, you can look up.

I heard Einstein said it, and there’s no way to prove it, if he really did (because lie spread too much is a truth to all, don’t believe me? Later!)

But here’s my viewpoint on this quote, it helps you know. Don’t memorize something you don’t need, you don’t feel important because you can always refer to Internet and books. You mind should be as free as possible, as relaxed as possible (like a kid) to think out of the box, to have ideas, to be creative.

Maybe that’s the reason for overthinking myself. It’s not that I don’t memorize on purpose but I don’t force on memorizing them, until they are permanent on their own. I try to be relax as much as possible (atleast try to), leading to too much ideas, that may be clashing and so. Ironically, this much free thinking (overclocking my brain in my language), leads to too much processes that needs to be done, occupying my memory. And as my Chemistry teacher used to say,

“Work cannot be Stored.”

(another blog on this later), you need to complete all your work, or they will be there unless you start completing them, or just trash them.

So the ideas in my mind (blogs that I wanted to write), are my work that needs to be done, overflowing my memory (buffer if you say so).

This blog is the reason to empty one of the process…
